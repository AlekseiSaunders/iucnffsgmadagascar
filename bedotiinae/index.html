Bedotia show great speciation among the eastern drainages of Madagascar, with every watershed having the possibility of containing a new species.

Nine described species and possibility 10 more undescibed species can be found in forested streams along the east coast.

Bedotia madagascariensis has the largest distribution of the genus, and was transplated into the Betsikiboka drainage in the 1950's.

Due to their preference for forested habitats, Bedotia are threatened throughout their ranges.

Bedotia sp nov 'Manombo' occurs in waters within Réserve Spéciale de Manombo 26km south of the coastal city of Farafangana along RN-12.

This species is considered Critically Endangered due to anthropogenic changes in the surrounding environment exacerbated by the narrow distribution range.

Bedotia leucopteron occurs in the middle reaches of Rianila River basin in eastern Madagascar. Type locality is Sandrakatrana Stream at Ampasimbe Village, Toamasina Province.

Bedotia longianalis is the only Bedotia found in rivers on Nosy Boraha. It is also found in the Ifontsy and Anove rivers on the mainland.

Bedotia albomarginata is found in the Mananara and Reinana river basins on the Eastern aspect of Madagascar. Type specimens were collected from the Sahapindra River near the towns of Vondrozo and Vevembe.

Bedotia marojejy is found in the Manantenina river to the south of Marojejy National Park. It is endangered in the wild with a decreasing population trend due to habitat loss.

Bedotia sp. nov. 'Namorona' is an undescibed species of found on the outskirts of Parc National Ranomafana.

Bedotia sp. nov. 'Garassa' is an undescibed species of found in the Sahamolotry river between Vangaindrano and Ranomena in southeastern Madagascar.

Rheocles vatosoa is found in the Lokoho river basin of northern Madagascar. The Lokoho River drains the southern half of the Marojejy Massif into the Indian Ocean.

Rheocles alaotrensis occurs in Lake Alotra, Maningory River system; Anjozorobe and in the tributaries of Betsikiboka on the west slope of Madagascar.
