Ptychochromis show their greatest diversity in the northern areas of Madagascar. Seven species are found in the northern quarter of the island.

Ptychochromis grandidieri has the largest distribution of the genus, extending from Fenoarivo in the north to at least Farafangana, if not further, to the south.

Ptychochromis onilahy, the only species yet discovered in the southwest, is presumed to be extinct.

Ptychochromis insolitus is a large cichlid from the Sofia drainage system in northwestern Madagascar. Thought to have gone extinct soon after its description it was re-discovered and brought into controlled propagation by a diverse team of local and internation conservationists. It is currently considered Critically Endangered.

Ptychochromis mainty is found in forested streams near Tôlanaro in the extreme southeast of Madagascar. Described by Martinez, Arroyave, and Sparks in 2015 little is known of its current conservation state in the wild.

Ptychochromis grandidieri has the largest range of any of the Ptychochromis species and is tolerant of brackish waters. It is considered a species of Least Concern in regards to conservation efforts.

Ptychochromis oligacantus is found in crater lakes on Nosy Be as well as the Sambirano river and north into the Mananjeba (Andranomaloto) drainage. This species is considered to be endangered with a decreasing population trend in the wild.
